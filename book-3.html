Chapter Two
Rhetoric & Propaganda

All three subjects employed a morphologically identical set of linguistic tools to restructure the perception of reality for their respective constituencies.

Delegitimising Information Channels

The mechanism involves making truth relative to the side one supports — until no independent information source remains credible.

Hitler · 1933
Lügenpresse

Characterised independent media as a "press of lies." Used to "synchronise" (Gleichschalten) the German press until no critical domestic voices remained.

The term predates Hitler but became the rhetorical anchor for eliminating press freedom entirely by 1934.
Lying Press — Wikipedia; Holocaust Encyclopedia
Trump · 2025–2026
"Fake News"

Consistently employs "Fake News" and "Enemy of the People." In March 2026, Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth explicitly chided media for reporting on military "negatives" — drone penetrations and casualties.

Echoes of Iraq: reporters blamed for "unhelpful" coverage of the Iran campaign — a structural repeat of 2003 embed management.
New Lines Magazine, March 2026
Netanyahu · 2022–2026
"Deep State"

Characterises legal challenges and political opposition as a "Deep State" plot designed to overthrow a democratically elected government.

Frames his corruption trials as an "antisemitic" attack on the Jewish state rather than an accountability process.
The American Prospect, March 2026

Chapter Three
Dismantling the Guardrails

The active subversion of judicial independence and the civil service is identified as the primary indicator of a transition from a constrained executive to an unconstrained one.

Germany · 1933
The Enabling Act

Passed March 23, 1933. Granted the cabinet power to bypass the Reichstag and the President to legislate. The deliberative legislature was effectively abolished in a single vote. Courts were subsequently "synchronised" (Gleichgeschaltet) — all critical judicial voices eliminated.

Judiciary: Removed
Legislature: Abolished
Civil Service: Purged

Source: Enabling Act of 1933 — Wikipedia; Holocaust Encyclopedia

USA · 2025–2026
Schedule F

Reclassifies tens of thousands of career civil servants as "at-will" employees — allowing arbitrary removal and replacement with loyalists. Officially renamed "Schedule Policy/Career."

In March 2026, Trump attacked the Supreme Court after Learning Resources, Inc. v. Trump struck down tariffs — calling Barrett and Gorsuch "unpatriotic" and stating they "should be ashamed of themselves."
SCOTUSblog, March 2026

Governs substantially by executive order and declared "faux-emergencies" — bypassing congressional deliberation.

Sources: NTEU; Democracy Forward; Government Executive, 2025–2026

The American Prospect, March 2026; CIE, January 2026
